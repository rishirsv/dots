# Lease Authoring Guidance (Executed-Style)

Use this guide when generating lease documents for data room simulations.

## Objective

Produce lease PDFs that read like negotiated commercial documents, not templates or summaries.

## Required Structure

Target about 10 pages per lease (unless user requests otherwise), using this sequence:

1. Title and parties
2. Defined terms
3. Demise/premises and permitted use
4. Lease term and rent commencement
5. Base rent and step-up mechanics
6. Additional rent and net lease terms (tax, CAM, insurance, utilities)
7. Insurance, indemnity, maintenance, casualty, condemnation
8. Assignment/subletting, lender protections, estoppel/notices
9. Default and remedies (with cure periods)
10. Signatures plus exhibits/addenda

## Required Clauses

Every lease should include these concepts:

- Effective date and named legal entities
- Legal description of premises (Exhibit A)
- Base rent amount and escalation schedule
- Additional rent categories and payment process
- Tenant and landlord maintenance responsibilities
- Insurance requirements and minimum limits
- Indemnification language
- Casualty/condemnation handling
- Assignment and subletting controls
- Event of default definitions and cure windows
- Notice addresses/methods
- Estoppel certificate obligation
- Governing law and attorney fee language

## Style Rules

- Use formal contract language and numbered sections.
- Keep terminology consistent (Landlord, Tenant, Premises, Lease Term, Base Rent, Additional Rent).
- Include realistic timing windows (e.g., 5-day monetary cure, 30-day non-monetary cure with diligent efforts).
- Include exhibits/addenda references in body and attach exhibit pages in the same PDF.
- Include page footer with page number.

## Realism Checks

Before finalizing each lease:

- Confirm rent escalators are arithmetically coherent year-to-year.
- Confirm term dates and escalation years align.
- Confirm exhibits referenced in body exist in the PDF.
- Confirm no placeholder text like `TBD`, `Lorem ipsum`, or missing party names.
- Confirm the lease reads as internally consistent legal prose.
