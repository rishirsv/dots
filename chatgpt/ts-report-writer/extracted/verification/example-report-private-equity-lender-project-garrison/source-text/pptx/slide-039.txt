Go forward structure
The operational guidelines set forth in KPMG’s Structure Slides of May 21, 2015 are intended to mitigate the risks of:
CII 4 establishing a PE in the U.S., which would otherwise result in an annual tax return filing requirement in the US and payment of tax on net income and gains; and
CPPIB from being attributed a commercial activity for purposes of section 892, which could eliminate the benefits available to CPPIB and certain of its Canadian subsidiaries under Section 892.
The key risk of this acquisition is primarily related to the maintenance of the structure going forward and the compliance with those guidelines.

Structure of the proposed transaction
The majority of the assets acquired
in this transaction are directly owned by GECC or its subsidiaries (“the Assets”).
If CII 4 is not able to acquire a California lending license prior to closing,
it is proposed that:
Bidco will acquire 100%
of the shares of Garrison, which will hold any assets that cannot be transferred to a Transfer Vehicle for legal or regulatory reasons.
Holdco or the SPVs will acquire 100% of the interests in the Transfer Vehicle(s) which will hold the broader Antares loan portfolio, the interests in relevant joint ventures, and Borrower Equity Co-Invests.
Blocker will acquire Equity Tags in portfolio companies that are structured as LLCs or partnerships, and that are engaged in a U.S. trade or business through a PE, as well as other non-loan assets that give rise to PE concerns (“Blocker Assets”).
Garrison will retain the revolving credit facilities and will continue to originate new loans for which a California lending license is necessary until CII 4 obtains its own license.
If CII 4 is able to acquire a California lending license prior to closing:
CII 4 will acquire 100% of the shares of Garrison.
Holdco or the SPVs will acquire 100% of the interests in the Transfer Vehicle(s).
Blocker will acquire the Blocker Assets.
From and after closing, all loan origination activity is undertaken by CII 4. Garrison will identify and source loans on behalf of CII 4 subject to operational guidelines.
