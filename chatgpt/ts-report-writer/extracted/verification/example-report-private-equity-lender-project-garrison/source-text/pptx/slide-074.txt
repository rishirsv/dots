Appendix 12
Illustrative loan economics
Event / Period
Accounting  Implications
January 1, 201x – loan is issued
Recognize the loan and corresponding cash outlay of $40 million
Recognize syndication fees of $2 million in “Upfront fees”
Closing fees received on hold amount ($400,000) and direct loan origination costs paid ($100,000) to be deferred
December 31, 201x – years 1 and 2
Recognize interest income ($2.2 million) over the period, and amortization of deferred closing fees ($80,000) and costs ($20,000) associated with the loan against yield
Amortization of deferred fees is recorded within “Deferred Fee Amortization”; amortization of deferred costs is recorded within “FAS 91 Expense Amortization” – both components of interest income
Recognize administration income ($50,000) earned during each year in “Other Fee Income”
December 31, 201x – year 3
Recognize interest income ($2.2 million) over the period, and amortization of closing fees ($80,000) and costs ($20,000) associated with the loan against yield
Recognize administration income ($50,000) earned during the year
Recognize cash receipts of $40 million and corresponding extinguishment of the loan
Recognize unamortized deferred closing fees ($160,000) and costs ($40,000) associated with the loan
Recognition of unamortized deferred fees in “Sweeps”; recognition of unamortized deferred costs in “FAS 91 Expense Amortization” – both components of interest income
If cash proceeds exceed book value of the loan due to any previously written-off amounts, difference between cash receipts and book value is recognized as a recovery
Fact Pattern of Sample Transaction
Cash-flow loan facility is agreed with customer - $100 million facility, benchmark index 3-month LIBOR (spread 4.5%, LIBOR floor 1.00%), stated term of 60 months, expected term of 36 months
Target is lead underwriter of the loan, or “left lead lender”
Syndication fee – 2% ($2 million)
Closing fee – 1% ($1 million), passed along to syndication participants pro-rata based on committed amounts
Target unreimbursed loan origination costs – $100,000
Loan administration fee to Target – $50,000 / year
Amount syndicated to third parties - $60 million
Target hold & fund - $40 million
Closing fee on the portion of loan retained must be deferred in order for the yield (exclusive of the syndication fee) to equal to the average yield obtained by syndication participants
