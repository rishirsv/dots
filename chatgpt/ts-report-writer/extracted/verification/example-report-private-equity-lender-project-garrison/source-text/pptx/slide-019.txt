Origination,
Syndication and New Business Returns (continued)
Target has experienced a gradual decline in NBM (defined as net interest
margin on new business divided by average net investment), from 4.61% in Q1’12 to 3.40% in Q1’15, with a corresponding decline in overall ROI. This decline is consistent with the shift toward lower-priced SSLP/MMGP loans (CFL NBM has been essentially flat). The exception to this trend was in Q1’14, when NBM increased 81 bps quarter-over-quarter (from 3.46% in Q4’13 to 4.27% in Q1’14). Management stated that this break was due to unusually low volumes in Q1’14 and the distorting impact of a few deals that were pricing outliers. Given that the quarter had the lowest origination volume of any quarter over the historical period ($2.2 billion, with $0.7 billion of on-book volume), the impact of this spike on portfolio ROI was minimal (an increase of 4 basis points).
In determining the profit-maximizing split between on-book volume and syndication on a go-forward basis, we recommend that in addition to the credit risk associated with retaining commitments on-book, you consider the fact that while yields are declining, they represent an ongoing source of revenue compared to one-time skim / upfront fees on syndications.
