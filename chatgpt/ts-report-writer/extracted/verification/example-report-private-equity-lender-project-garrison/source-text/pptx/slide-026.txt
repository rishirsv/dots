Equity co-investments
Target has made equity investments in 168
borrowers totaling $475 million as at March 31, 2015 to provide another source of income and help facilitate client relationships. These investments are diversified across various sectors such that there is minimal industry concentration risk.
The typical equity investment is between $3 and $7 million, and generally represents an ownership interest between 0.5% and 3.0%. The average investment amount and ownership interest across the portfolio is $2.5 million and 2.3%, respectively.
Of the 168 equity investments as at March 31, 2015, only 2 had an ownership stake greater than 10%; the largest of which is a 20.5% interest (valued at $13 million) in a trucking company. The majority of larger investments result from troubled loan workouts.
Approximately 62% of equity investments have been valued with an EBITDA multiple of between 9.0-12.0x. EBITDA multiples within industries / sub-industries are fairly consistent with the industry / sub-industry average.
Gains from equity investments, which are recognized upon sale of the investment, over the past nine quarters have ranged from $17 million (in Q1’14) to $44 million in (Q4’14).
Management
stated they believe the existing portfolio currently has approximately $300 million of embedded gains that are forecasted to be recognized to earnings as the investments are sold over the next three years.
