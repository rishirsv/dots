Appendix 18
Supporting analysis of tax key findings (3)
Tax Audit Status, Warrants & Liens
GE
participates in the IRS’s compliance assurance process (“CAP”) program.  Under the CAP program, GE is subject to ongoing federal examination, and often times significant federal income tax issues are discussed with IRS examiners prior to tax return filing.  Taxable years since 2010 are currently open for audit due to the ongoing audit cycle of GE.
Target Management
has represented that no specific audit issues related to federal tax matters are
outstanding and Management is not aware of any federal IDR’s that pertain to Target.
Target Management has represented that they are not aware of any ongoing state income tax examinations that involve Target. Management has provided a listing of notices and correspondence with state income tax authorities for tax years 2010 through 2013. The notices and correspondence related to immaterial state income and franchise tax adjustments and various information requests. No significant issues were identified.
Management stated that Target is currently corresponding with the Canadian Revenue Agency to cancel its Canadian Tax ID as it is not required to file tax returns in Canada.
Related Party Transactions
The
Target
has entered into (i) a revolving credit agreement with an affiliate of GE and (ii) an omnibus loan agreement between GECC and its subsidiaries for intercompany loans.  Management has stated that both agreements are consistent with GE transfer pricing practices, which attempts to satisfy arm’s length pricing.
As
of May of 2015, Target has a net intercompany receivable balance of approximately $150 million, which consists of $350 million of intercompany receivables and $200 million of intercompany payables.
Target has also entered into a master service agreement with GECC for certain services that GECC provides to Target. Management has represented that the agreement is consistent with GE transfer pricing practices.
Management has represented that with the exception of the revolving credit agreement, the omnibus loan agreement, and the master services agreement, Target has not entered into any other related party transactions.
As
these transactions have occurred with other US entities within GE’s tax consolidated group, they are unlikely to have created a material federal tax exposure, even if sufficient transfer pricing documentation has not been maintained.
However
, there is a state tax risk if arm’s length terms have not been applied to these transactions where both parties are not included in the same combined returns for state income tax purposes.
