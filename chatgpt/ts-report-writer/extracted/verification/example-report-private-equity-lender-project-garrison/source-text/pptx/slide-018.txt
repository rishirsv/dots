Syndication and New Business Returns
O
riginations in 2014 totaled $17 billion, compared to $18.2 billion in 2013. Management has attributed declining origination volumes to Parent’s conservative interpretation of new leverage lending guidelines issued by banking regulators and a general slowdown in middle-market M&A activity, particularly in Q1’14.
Of the volume originated in 2014, 50%, or $8.5 billion, was syndicated, as compared to 43%, or $8.0 billion, in 2013. The increase in syndication volume drove higher upfront / skim fees for the year ($172 million in 2014 versus $158 million in 2013). During 2014, syndication volume was relatively consistent from Q1 to Q3 and higher in Q4, resulting in higher upfront / skim fees in the latter part of the year.
