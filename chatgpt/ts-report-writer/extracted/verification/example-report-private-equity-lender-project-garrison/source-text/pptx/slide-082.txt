Appendix 18
Supporting analysis of tax key findings (2)
Significant Tax Positions and Elections
Management
stated that GE and GECC do not carry any reserves for uncertain tax positions under ASC 740-10 (FIN 48) or ASC 450-20 (FAS 5) on their financial statements that pertain to the Target.
We
have not received Target’s standalone tax provision
or state apportionment work
papers for any tax year to verify this point.  We recommend
these items are received
and confirmed prior to signing.
However
, based on the foregoing points and level of activity, the quantum of historical tax risk exposures are likely to be limited.
Management
stated the Target has historically undertaken certain reportable transactions relating to losses incurred on debt instruments.  For example, if a taxpayer claims a loss under section 165 and the loss exceeds a certain threshold (which depends on the U.S. federal tax classification of the entity), then the taxpayer must report the loss on Form 8886
.  Management
has represented that Target had one reportable transaction relating to a loss on the sale of a debt instrument in excess of $10 million.  These transactions are fairly routine for this type of business and the existence of a reportable transaction should not on its own heighten the Target’s tax risk.
Management
stated the Target has not entered in to any listed transactions.
In
tax year 2009, the Target, as a dealer in certain securities, elected to recognize gain or loss on certain securities using the mark-to-market method of accounting under section 475.
Effective Tax Rate
Management
has estimated the Target’s ETR to be 38%.
This rate represents a federal tax rate of 35% and a state tax rate of approx. 3%.
This
approximate ETR seems slightly low, as we would expect the ETR when combining state and federal taxes to be approximately 40-42%.
Management
noted that this is a rough estimate and that they would provide state apportionment workpapers to assist in solidifying these numbers.
