Refinancing volume:
increased significantly in Q2 2014 as a result of the reduction in NBM from 4.42% to 3.43% from Q1 to Q2. Refinancing volumes after Q2 2014 reverted to an average of approximately $260 million per quarter.
Refinancing runoff:
represents the cash flows foregone as a result of borrowers refinancing their loans with Antares or through another lender. Refinancing volumes and refinancing run-offs broadly off-set each other, therefore most growth in ENI is represented through new customers or line increases.
Payouts:
represents payouts of loans by borrowers. Payouts increased from $426 million in Q1 2014 to $1,548M in Q4 2014. Payouts reverted back to $1,030 million in Q1 2015. We note that payouts broadly track new volumes by quarter.
Other items:
includes changes during the year related to amortization, held for sale securities, Antares revolvers, taxes payable, and other items. Of note is a deferred tax asset of which increased ENI by $518 million in Q4 2014.
