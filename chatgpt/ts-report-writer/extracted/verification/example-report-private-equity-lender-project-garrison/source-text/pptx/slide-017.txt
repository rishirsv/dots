Reference
Carve-out / shared services
A number of back-office support functions
– IT, operations, finance, legal, marketing, HR –
are provided to Target via shared services
centers. These centers are necessary to the
operation of Target’s business activities, and services provided by these centers will need to be performed in-house or outsourced to third-party vendors post-transaction. We recommend you consider potential incremental costs, both one-time and recurring, associated with the separation from the Parent and subsequently establishing these functions
in order for Target to operate as a stand-alone entity.
We acknowledge you are working with advisors on the potential one-time costs and synergies from the proposed transaction.
In
2014, Target recognized a total of $41 million of costs
allocated by the Parent to Target for
centrally-provided services, up from $38 million in 2013
. These charges accounted for 19% of SG&A costs in both 2013 and 2014.
They relate primarily to IT (the maintenance and provision of centrally-managed IT systems), Operations (transaction processing and ancillary services to support sales and execution), and Capital Markets (support services relating to loan distribution and associated capital markets activities). Management
indicated they have not been provided transparency by Parent as to the basis of allocation of these costs.
Management has noted that the current IT platform is inefficient and the optimization of this platform will yield cost savings. We recommend that you consider the following:
IT platform optimization will involve
one-time
setup costs;
Applications developed specifically for Target will be transferred as
part of the separation
based on a perpetual license. Diligence is suggested
on the
knowledge transfer, flight risk and quality of existing/planned documentation; and
Target will have to make decisions around whether to transfer over existing shared IT staff or to recruit new personnel to replicate services provided by such staff.
Compensation and benefits made up 38%
of
IT costs in 2014.
Management has also noted that there are potential savings from downsizing the Operations support team (not yet quantified) and other shared services functions (accounting and legal support, with projected annual savings of approximately
$3 million).
We recommend that you consider whether a downsized Operations team will have the capacity to handle back-end processes to support the growth in deal flow forecasted by Management.
More information is required as to the specific accounting and legal support provided by the Parent. Pending such information, we recommend that you consider the experience, competence and current commitments of the existing core legal and accounting teams as well as their ability to absorb additional responsibilities without headcount additions.
