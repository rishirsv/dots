Unfunded and funded commitments (continued)
Average funded assets have increased from $14.6 billion in 2012 to $16.0 billion during Q1’15, primarily due to the growth in the SSLP and MMGP programs (from an aggregate of $4.1 billion as at December 31, 2012 to $6.0 billion as at March 31, 2015). The launch of the MMGP joint venture in 2014 added $0.4 billion to the portfolio during the year, with forecasts of continued ramp-up of the program in 2015.
Growth in the SSLP and MMGP programs was offset by a $1.9 billion decline from 2012 to 2014 in the Core portfolio origination volume. Management stated that the decrease was due to the conservative interpretation of new leveraged lending guidelines issued by banking regulators and a slowdown in middle-market M&A activity in 2014. On a portfolio-wide basis, originations decreased from $18.8 billion in 2012 to $17.0 billion in 2014. During Q1’15, the SSLP portfolio remained flat, the MMGP portfolio increased by $0.1 billion, and the Core portfolio increased by $0.2 billion.
Any expansion of the hold size in Target’s current core market or into new sectors post-transaction as contemplated by Management will require additional sources of funding to meet larger commitments.
Assets held-for-sale are loans held temporarily on Target’s books until they are sold down to syndication partners. These assets, which are carried at the lower of cost or fair value, can take between 30 and 90 days to sell, but are typically sold within 30 days. Over the historical period being analyzed, the highest quarter-end balance held by Target was $0.5 billion (as at December 31, 2014). You should consider the short-term working capital needs associated with these held-for-sale assets.
