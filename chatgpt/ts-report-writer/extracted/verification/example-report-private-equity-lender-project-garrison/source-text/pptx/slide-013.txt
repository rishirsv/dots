Compensation and benefit expense is
Target’s largest expense, comprising
71% and 69% of SG&A expense in 2013 and 2014, respectively. It includes a base component and a profit-driven variable non-deferred cash bonus paid
annually
.
Compensation and benefit expense increased
from
$140.6 million
in 2013 to $151.8 million in 2014, primarily due to an 18% year-over-year increase in headcount. The additions were substantially in
Commercial, at the direction of GE to drive volume growth, and Risk, in response to the evolving regulatory environment.
Variable compensation makes up a significant portion of total compensation in all functions. The proportion is particularly high in income-generating functions such as Commercial and Capital Markets, where variable compensation represents an average of 55% and 43%, respectively, of total employee compensation.
