CV% by Product and Deal Type
CV%, which represents CV (contribution value, defined as total interest, fee and other income less interest expense) divided by average earning assets (that is, financing receivables including SSLP receivables), is a key metric tracked by management. CV% on CFL is consistently higher than CV% on SSLP / MMGP loans, primarily because the additional fee income earned on SSLP / MMGP loans is more than offset by the higher interest income yields on CFL relative to SSLP / MMGP. Consistent with the recommendations above, we recommend that you consider the benefit from growth in the SSLP / MMGP portfolios concurrently with the lower CV profile of these assets.
CV% on line increases and refinancing tend to trend together, whereas CV% of new deals tends to fluctuate above or below the percentages for line increases and refinancing. While NBM for new deals was considerably lower than NBM for line increases and refinancing, the differential is reduced for CV% because new deals generate significant fee income through syndications which makes up for the shortfall in interest income yield. We note, however, that the CV% for new deals is contingent on the continuing ability to assume lead arranger roles in syndications, whereas the CV% for line increases and refinancing is driven primarily by higher interest income yield.
