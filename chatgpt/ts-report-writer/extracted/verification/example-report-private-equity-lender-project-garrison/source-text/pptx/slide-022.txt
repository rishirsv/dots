Executive summary
Key findings: financial (11)
Unused Commitment Fees
Unused commitment fees are assessed on the unused portion of terms loans or revolvers and are calculated as a contractually-stated percentage of the unused portion of such facilities. Such fees are a means to partially recover the costs associated with standby liquidity facilities that allow Target to fund additional draws on a timely basis.
Commitment fees have remained relatively flat over the historical period. Since 2008, annual unused commitment fees have averaged $24 million, with a maximum of $28 million (2010) and a minimum of $20 million (2008). Fees are a product of unfunded commitments, which have been relatively flat over the historical period (refer to discussion on “Unfunded and funded commitments”). In general, utilization of revolvers tends to decrease (that is, unfunded amounts tend to increase) in times of strong economic growth and excess liquidity.
Syndication Fees
Syndication fees are earned from borrowers on syndicated loans originated by Target and for which Target acts in the capacity of lead arranger. Of the cash fees received, a portion is recognized upfront in income (“Upfront Fees”) and the residual amount is deferred and amortized into income over the term of the loan (“Deferred Fees”). If and when a loan prepays or is refinanced, any unamortized deferred fees relating to that loan are “swept” into income (“Sweeps”).
