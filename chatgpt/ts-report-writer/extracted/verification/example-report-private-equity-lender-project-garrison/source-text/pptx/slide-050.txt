We were able to agree aggregate balances (Total Revenue, Net Revenue and Net Income) from Target’s reported financial information to Hyperion system-generated reports. For specific line items, we were able to agree reported information to Hyperion reports except as noted below. We note that for some balances, such as Letter of Credit fees and Admin Fees, the amounts were agreed to GL accounts that were only used pre-2012 and have nil balances in 2012 and after (i.e., the equivalent balances are recorded in alternative accounts 2012 onwards). These accounts were kept within Hyperion to allow historical comparisons to be made. We read the account descriptions and noted that the descriptions were consistent with the nature of the balances being reported.

Provision for Credit Losses (2008 to 2014)
The provision for credit losses is booked to the GL via manual journal entries on an asset-by-asset basis. We obtained a document, redacted for borrower names, showing the quarterly and annual provision amounts, both by borrower and in aggregate. The total provision amount per this document agreed to the total reported provision amounts for each of 2008 through 2014. We compared this document to a Hyperion system-generated report pulled by management and verified that both the individual and total amounts per this source document agreed with the amounts per Hyperion.
