Supporting analysis of tax key findings (5)
Acquisition Structure and Mechanics
The
Seller’s Proposed Acquisition
Structure:
Target
Management suggested that prior to the proposed acquisition of the Target, the targeted assets would be (i) contributed from GECC to Target or (ii) sold by other subsidiaries of GECC to Target prior to the proposed acquisition of Target.  In addition, the targeted employees would be transferred to Target.
Regarding
the proposed transaction structure, Target Management stated:
The
Target holds a California lending license and certain of the targeted loans and revolvers must be held by an entity with such a license.
The
contribution and sale of the targeted assets to the Target should be a “taxable” transaction to the Seller, and thus, should result in Target having a tax basis in such assets equal their respective fair market values.
We believe this should be correct.
Proposed
Alternative Acquisition Structure
Target
Management stated that the Seller is flexible and open to alternative acquisition structures that may prove beneficial to the
parties.  The Structuring Slides provided by Debevoise
provide significantly more detail on the alternative proposed by CPPIB.  However, it is broadly expected that:
GECC contributes and GECC subsidiaries transfer pursuant to an intercompany sale to the Target any loans and revolvers that must be held by an entity with a California Lenders License.
GECC also transfers employment of the Target Employees to Garrison.
