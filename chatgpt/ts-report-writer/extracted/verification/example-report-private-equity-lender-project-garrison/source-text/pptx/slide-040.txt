Appendix 18
Tax attributes & deconsolidation
Upon the acquisition of Target, Target will cease to be a member of the GE US tax consolidated group.  As a result, the tax attributes of the group, including group losses, should be distributed back to individual group members.
A determination of tax attributes separately allocable to Target post-acquisition will need to be made.  Although Target Management has represented that Target has no federal NOL carryovers, a small state NOL balance,
no
capital loss carryovers, and accumulated E&P
as at 12/31/14 of $19M,
these representations should be confirmed prior to signing.
Target will be required to file a short-year tax return for federal and potentially state tax purposes for the period following its deconsolidation from the GE US tax consolidated group.
Target will remain liable for tax liabilities which relate to tax years in which Target was a member of the GE US consolidated tax group.  As discussed above, however, the quantum of historical tax exposures is likely to be small.
