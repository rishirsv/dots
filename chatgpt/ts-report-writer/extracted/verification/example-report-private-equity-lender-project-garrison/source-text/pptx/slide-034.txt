Bank Loan Group
The Bank Loan Group, or BLG, is a business unit of GE Capital Corporation that in
vests primarily in non-investment-grade, broadly syndicated bank loans, typically greater than $150 million in size. The business had earnings of $82 million in 2014, with assets yielding 4.5% and loan losses of 130 bps during the period.
BLG, which is managed together with Target, invests in U.S. syndicated bank loan deals in the primary and secondary market with the following characteristics:
Non-investment grade, broadly syndicated, senior bank loans
More than $250mm typical deal size with ~$150mm average EBITDA
For business expansions, acquisitions, recapitalization, LBOs, and general refinancing
Trade exposures approximate $20 million
Management stated BLG’s ability to generate earnings has been adversely affected by GE’s conservative investment strategy coming out of the financial crisis and interpretation of the new leverage lending guidelines issued in 2013.
In 2008, the business had $16.7 billion of funded assets and 96 employees, as compared to $3.3 billion of funded assets and 46 employees as at March 31, 2015.
Management stated that the current team can be scalable in respect to investments in senior secured credit facilities; however, expertise would be necessary to meaningfully grow the distressed debt portfolio. Additionally, BLG does not currently invest within the health care and energy sectors. Expansion into the those sectors would require investment to obtain the necessary specialized expertise.
Our due diligence procedures on BLG are based on the limited data on BLG received to date.
