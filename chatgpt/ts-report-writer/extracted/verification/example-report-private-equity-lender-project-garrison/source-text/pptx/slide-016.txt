Employee retention arrangements
Under the GE Capital Disposition Retention Program (the “Retention Program”), Target has retention agreements in place with certain employees across various functions who have been identified as critical to the success of the business post-transaction.
Under these arrangements, eligible employees earn a retention award equal to a contractually-stated percentage of their annual base salary in effect on April 1, 2015. The cumulative retention award across all functions is $51 million. The first 70% of this award will be earned upon the close of sale and the remaining 30% will be earned upon the six-month anniversary of the employees’ direct transfer to the buyer (the “70-30 arrangement”). Both amounts will be paid within 60 days of being earned. The Retention Program covers approximately 90% of the senior leadership team, between 10 and 20 originators, an unspecified percentage of other Commercial employees, approximately 45% of Risk and Capital Markets employees, and approximately 25% of all other employees.
We recommend you consider whether this payout structure provides key employees with a sufficiently long-term incentive to continue their employment with the business post-acquisition.
Approximately 40% of the top originators have been offered enrollment in a Guaranteed Sales Incentive Compensation Program (the “GSP”). The regular-way Sales Incentive Compensation (“SIC”) plan is a bonus plan that rewards originators based on sales volumes, net deal profit and sponsor / customer relationships. Under the GSP, originators are guaranteed a minimum SIC payment amount even if their normal SIC payments calculates to a lower amount. Payments totaling $18.9 million (in aggregate) have been guaranteed for 2015 under the GSP.
We recommend that you consider whether the GSP sufficiently incentivizes top performers to remain with the business given the central role originators play in driving new business.
In addition to the above, approximately 25 executives spread over various functions will receive advance vesting of their supplemental pension plan upon closing of sale and continued employment with a successor employer. The aggregate net present value of the benefit payable when the executives reach age 60 is approximately $102.7 million.
It is our understanding that all retention awards and payments pursuant to advance vesting of the supplementary pension plan are the Parent’s obligation.
