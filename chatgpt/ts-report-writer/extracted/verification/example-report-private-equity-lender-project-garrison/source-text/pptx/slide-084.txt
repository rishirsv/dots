Supporting analysis of tax key findings (4)
Tax Function & Transition Consideration
Although
the Target has approximately 25 back office professionals that include individuals that assist with tax compliance, these individuals focus primarily on federal income tax issues.
Affiliates
of GECC provide the tax compliance and reporting support to the Target in the following areas:
State
income and franchise taxes;
Indirect
state taxes, including sales and use and property taxes (noting that Target Management has stated that Target does not file any personal property tax returns);
Payroll
taxes (noting that Target Management has stated that Target has not historically had any employees, and therefore no payroll tax return filing requirement); and
Withholding
taxes and information reporting.
Until
suitable internal or external resources can be located, it will be critical to the smooth transition of this acquisition that a transition services agreement covering the full profile of tax compliance and reporting functions not available within the Target is negotiated and concluded.
That
agreement should also cover the quantum of historical information CPPIB will receive at acquisition and sufficient access to historical records and resources within GE, GECC, or their affiliates to support any defense of an income or indirect tax audit for federal and state tax purposes.
Foreign Investment in Real Property Act of 1980 (“FIRPTA”)
If
following the acquisition of the Target, Holdco or the SPVs were to dispose of certain
loans (which includes making significant modifications to terms) CII
4 could potentially be subject to US tax on the gain from such disposition under FIRPTA.
Only
approximately 0.8% of the Target’s investment portfolio (debt and equity) relates to investments in finance, insurance, and real estate combined. Moreover, Target Management has represented that to the best of its knowledge, none of the debt investments provide a right to share in appreciation of or profits from US real estate.  We recommend that
this
representation be confirmed prior to signing.
Following the acquisition, we recommend that certain controls are established to minimize the US tax exposure relating to any subsequent dispositions of US real property interests.
