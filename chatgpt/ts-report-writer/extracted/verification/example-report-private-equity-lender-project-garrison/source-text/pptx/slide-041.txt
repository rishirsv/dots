Tax function & transition services agreement
Although the Target has approx. 25 back office professionals that include individuals that assist with tax compliance, these individuals focus primarily on federal income tax issues.  Affiliates of GECC provide the tax compliance and reporting support to the Target in areas such as state income and franchise taxes; indirect state taxes, payroll taxes, and withholding taxes and information reporting.
Until suitable internal or external resources can be located, it will be critical to the smooth transition of this acquisition that a transition services agreement covering the full profile of tax compliance and reporting functions not available within the Target is negotiated and concluded.
The transition services agreement should also cover the quantum of historical information CPPIB will receive at acquisition and sufficient access to historical records and resources within GE, GECC, or their affiliates to support any defense of an income or indirect tax audit for federal and state tax purposes.
Appendix 18
Debt/Equity and FIRPTA
If CII 4 or Holdco were to purchase debt investments that were treated as equity for US tax purposes and that equity was held in a partnership that was engaged in a US trade or business (“USTB”), CII 4 would be subject to tax on income from that investment in the US on a net basis.
In addition, if the interest on any loans is tied to the profits or appreciation in value of US real estate the loan should be treated as a FIRPTA asset and US net basis taxation may result on any gain on sale or modification of the debt.
If such loans are to be purchased, it may be more suitable to have them be purchased by a new US C Corporation (“Blocker”) to mitigate any risk to CII 4.
Based on our review, the nature of the debt investments to be acquired are unlikely to cause a concern that such investments should be treated as equity for US federal income tax purposes. In addition, the nature and quantum of the debt investments do not appear to create any material FIRPTA risk, e.g.,
approx. 0.8% of the Target’s investment portfolio (debt and equity) relates to investments in finance, insurance, and real estate combined.
Target Management has represented that none of the targeted debt investments contain terms that may cause the investment to be at risk of not being treated as debt for US tax purposes.  Moreover, Target Management has represented that to the best of its knowledge, none of the debt investments provide a right to share in appreciation of or profits from US real estate.  We recommend that these representations be confirmed prior to signing.
