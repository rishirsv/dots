Purchase accounting adjustments
We understand that an AcquirorCo would be set up to effect the acquisition of Target. The proposed transaction would be considered a business combination in the accounting records of AcquirorCo; therefore, the acquired assets and any assumed liabilities will be adjusted to their respective fair market values at the acquisition date. The difference between the purchase price and the fair value of the net tangible and other intangible assets (i.e., customer relationships) is attributed to goodwill. The application of push-down accounting would not be applicable as AcquirorCo would be acquiring specific assets rather than shares of a legal entity.
It is our understanding there is a chance that CII 4 consolidates
AcquirorCo
. If it does not, then it would account for its investment in
AcquirorCo
under the equity-method accounting (assuming significant influence, but lack of control) for its proportionate share of income/loss, and its initial investment would be recorded at fair market value as part of purchase accounting.
Goodwill is not amortized, but is subject to annual evaluation for impairment. Other intangible assets are amortized to earnings on a straight-line basis over their expected useful life. The impact of the amortization expense associated with the value assigned to Target’s customer relationships should be considered in your transaction model.
Equity investments will be revalued to their respective fair market values and subsequently accounted for under the cost- or equity-method based on its level of influence, consistent with historical practice.
As a result of the purchase accounting adjustments, the expected positive mark-to-market adjustment to the equity co-investments will result in a decline in future gains on the existing portfolio as reported on the income statement of AcquirorCo for a period of time and an increased potential for impairment charges. Management stated that the existing portfolio currently has approximately $300 million of embedded gains that are forecasted to be recognized in earnings as the investments are sold over the next three years.
Also as a result of purchase accounting, any deferred fees and costs will be included in the post-transaction cost base of the associated loans acquired once they are revalued to fair value in the books of AcquirorCo (but will remain in Target’s books). Existing deferred amounts will therefore no longer be amortized into income.
Target’s allowance for loan losses does not carry over in the acquisition in the book of AcquirorCo (yet to remain in Target’s books). Instead, all acquired loans shall initially be recorded at fair value without a reserve.
