Upfront Fees
Fees relating to the portion of the total commitment funded by (or “sold down” to) syndication participants, regardless of whether the syndicated transaction is classified as a “Loan Syndication” or a “Loan Participation” for accounting purposes, are recognized upfront (i.e. when cash is received). As well, fees from transactions classified as “Loan Syndications” that represent “above-market” fees earned on the portion of the total commitment retained by Target are also recognized upfront.
In 2014, upfront fees totaled $172 million, up from $158 million in 2013 due to higher syndication volumes in the year ($8.5 billion, versus $8.0 billion in 2013). In both 2013 and 2014, 80% of total upfront fees related to the Core portfolio (including Franchise but excluding the SSLP and MMGP portfolios). In Q1’15, the ratio
fell to 69%, primarily due to the growth of the SSLP and MMGP portfolios relative to the Core portfolio.
Within the Core portfolio, cash-flow loan (“CFL”) syndications constitute the majority of the total balance syndicated (of the $8.5 billion syndicated in 2014, $8.4 billion related to the Core portfolio; of this $8.4 billion, $8.3 billion related to CFLs), with asset-based loan (“ABL”) syndications making up the remainder. While this is in part driven by the fact that CFLs make up most of the Core portfolio volume originated (96% in 2014), we note that a greater proportion of CFLs originated are syndicated by Target (65%, compared to 25% of ABL loans). This is because ABLs tend to command higher yields (providing an incentive to increase the “hold” on these loans) and because, in a recovering economy, capital markets are more receptive to CFLs than ABLs. We recommend that you consider these trends and relationships as part of your valuation model.
Upfront fees earned closely
track syndication volumes. From 2010 to 2012, volumes syndicated increased from $3.4 billion to $8.1 billion, driven both by an increase in total volume originated (from $9.6 billion in 2010 to $18.8 billion in 2012) and an increase in the percentage of total originated volume that was syndicated (from approximately 36% in 2010 to approximately 43% in 2012). Originated and syndication volumes were relatively flat in 2012 and 2013. In 2014, while volume originated decreased by $1.4 billion relative to 2013, volume syndicated increased by $0.5 billion (from 43% of total volume in 2013 to 50% of total volume in 2014). Upfront fees have followed the same trend – an increase from 2010 ($82 million) to 2012 ($159 million), a flattening out from 2012 to 2013 ($158 million) and a further increase in 2014 ($172 million).
