Financial Reporting Systems / Infrastructure
Source Systems
Source systems used by Target to record loans and other transactions include ACBS (a vendor-supplied and customizable loan recording / accounting system used in the industry), Venture (to record equity investments), AP (payables), TE (to record travel and entertainment expenses) and
Cognos
(to record miscellaneous expenses and treasury-related activities). These systems interface directly with Target’s Oracle-based general ledger system.
Manual Journal Entries
Within the GL system, manual journal entries (“MJEs”) are posted to balances from source systems in order to ensure that reported balances are consistent with accrual-based accounting guidelines. Major classes of MJEs include:
Volume accruals: There is usually a lag between the execution of a transaction and the time it is input into the ACBS system as loans underwritten on or close to the last day of the period are sometimes not entered into ACBS by the Operations team until after period end. To ensure these transactions are reflected in the financial records for the period, manual entries are made to account for the loans (both on the balance sheet and related income statement accounts). These entries are based on source documentation (such as loan agreements). Management has stated that it was not aware of instances when the amounts accrued via an MJE were inconsistent with the amount subsequently determined by ACBS once Operations has input the transaction data into ACBS.
Expense accruals: Accruals for expenses incurred, but not paid in a given reporting period.
ALLL-related entries: Subsequent to the determination by a dedicated ALLL COE team that specific assets are to be classified as non-earning, an adjustment must be made within ACBS to ensure that interest income is no longer being accrued on these assets and, if applicable, interest payments are being applied to principal reduction. When this determination is made on or close to period end, ACBS is typically not updated until after period-end. Manual entries are made to the general ledger to reflect these changes. According to Management, subsequent to the ACBS update, an ACBS report listing non-earning assets is compared to both the original ALLL COE list and the journal entries to ensure consistency.
Unapplied cash: Cash received on or close to the last day of the period is not applied to loans until after period end date. An entry is made to apply this cash. At the same time, an offsetting entry is made to an off-book suspense account. When received cash is applied to loans post-period-end by the ACBS system, the off-book suspense account is cleared. We noted that, except for stale balances that are not significant in nature or amount, the historical balances in this suspense account are cleared shortly after period end.
Provision for credit losses: Provisions are booked to the GL on an asset-by-asset basis. See below for specific procedures performed on reported provision amounts.
Hyperion
Hyperion is a financial reporting tool that sits on top of the GL and is used by Target management to prepare financial records for financial planning and analysis and legal entity reporting to the Parent. Hyperion has the functional capability to assign “BSLA” tags to specific accounts and/or deals. These tags are used to differentiate between SF and non-SF balances/deals, and are the key means by which SF balances are aggregated for financial reporting purposes
