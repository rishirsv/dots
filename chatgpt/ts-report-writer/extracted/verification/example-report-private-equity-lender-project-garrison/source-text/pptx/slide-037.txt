FAS
91 adjustment for preceding years:
FAS 91 requires direct loan origination fees and costs to be deferred and amortized to interest income over the term of each loan. Target began deferring and amortizing these costs in 2013 (the deferrals were historically made at the Parent-entity level)
on a prospective basis and
, as a result, the amortization amount reported in Target’s books is not reflective of the true earnings impact. We are unable to quantify this adjustment in the absence of information relating to amounts deferred; however, if the 2015 forecast is used as a proxy for normal annual amortization, the adjustments to reflect the retroactive adoption of FAS 91 would be a $46 million reduction to interest income in 2013 and a $22 million reduction to interest income in 2014.
