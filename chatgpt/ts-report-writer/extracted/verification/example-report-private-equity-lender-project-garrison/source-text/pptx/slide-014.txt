People matters (continued)
Commercial Team
The average and total compensation in the Commercial function is significantly higher relative to other functions (Commercial comprised approximately 50% of total compensation in 2013 and 2014) due to its relative importance in driving volume and maintaining client relationships. A strong commercial team is necessary to drive both new loan originations and
refinancings
in order to replenish a loan portfolio that, by its nature, has a relatively short expected life. Average origination volume per FTE in the
Commercial function was $205 million in 2013 and $172 million
in 2014.
Bonuses awarded to the Commercial team are primarily based on profits earned over the term of a loan and involve a discretionary component based on the development of client relationships. Within the Commercial function, senior employees (SVP and above) on average earn more than 50% and up to 75% of their total compensation in the form of variable compensation. The compensation structure is intended to reflect volume generated and depth of client relationships maintained.
Given the importance of the Commercial function to Target’s ability to generate earnings, we recommend that you consider whether the current compensation structure with no bonus deferral mechanism provides key employees with sufficient and adequate incentives to continue with the business in the longer term. As well, given that a vast majority of deals are sourced from existing customers (76% and 74% in 2013 and 2014, respectively), we recommend that you consider whether the total compensation package offered to top-performing originators is sufficient to retain those with long-standing client relationships.
Other
As a result of the proposed transaction, which may
involve moving to an unregulated ownership model, Target is projecting a reduction of 88 FTEs (50 in Risk, 32 in Commercial and 6 in other functions); these FTEs represent 27% of the headcount as at March 31, 2015. The headcount reduction is expected to generate annual savings of approximately $33 million. Management indicated that a portion of these savings would need to be redirected towards retention of key remaining employees. There have been significant headcount additions over the course of 2014 (an increase of approximately 50 FTEs or
18
%), primarily to enhance regulatory compliance but also to drive asset and income growth. You should consider whether the projected cost savings to be achieved from these previously discussed headcount reductions would be sustainable if forecasted asset growth is to be achieved, particularly if the achievement of such growth requires Target to move to new sectors and/or larger deals where relevant experience would be required and which management indicated Target may not currently have. We understand that you have engaged with advisors to analyze this matter further.
You should also consider the timing and quantum of, and responsibility for, any potential severance payments as a negotiation point between you and the seller.
