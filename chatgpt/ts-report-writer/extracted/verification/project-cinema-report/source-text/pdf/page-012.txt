Film cost: Increased from $114.7 million in FY21 to $322.1 million in the TTM Mar-24 period. Additionally, film cost increased from 48.0% to 53.0% of box office revenue throughout the scope period
due to lowered negotiation power regarding licensing costs. However, Q1-24 saw lower film costs due to top films in the current period having lower settlement rates.
Cost of food service: Increased from $41.7 million in FY21 to $114.3 million in the TTM Mar-24 period, representing a 174.1% increase. This growth is primarily due to the associated growth in food
service revenue. LBE locations saw a $9.4 million increase in cost of food service, which is consistent with its 236% increase in associated food service revenue.
Total other cost: Employee wages and salaries saw an increase of $22.9 million across the scope period. Management states that this is due to an increase in demand for employees as theatre
attendance levels increase. Furthermore, the Company saw a $15.5 million increase in purchased services, as well as a $17.2 million increase in advertising and promotion which aligns with pre-
COVID levels.
EBITDA margin: The Company’s EBITDA margins increased by 33.24% from FY21 to FY22, and by 9.27% from FY22 to FY23. However, EBITDA margins decreased by 0.43% from FY23 to TTM
Mar-24. This is due to the $18.1 million decrease in EBITDA from Q4-23 to Q1-24 due to release delays as a result of the Writers Guild of America strike.
