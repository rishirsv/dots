Net working capital (5/5) – Other considerations
Other considerations
The following items have been discussed by Management and should be considered for future events which may impact working capital.
A.     Cadillac Fairview contracts: As part of the Company’s digital place-based media segment, a contract was penned with Cadillac Fairview to operate a network of 200 digital displays in 18
Cadillac Fairview shopping centres, and to sell digital and static media, and sponsorships, for its extensive network of shopping destinations across Canada. Operations have begun in Q1-24
but management has not specified any net working capital associated with this contract.
B.     Lost contracts within digital place-based media: Management did not specify lost contracts. Pending more detail information on any lost contracts within the scope period, an analysis
would be performed.
C.     Accrued vacation: The current balance of accrued vacation paid to employees is non-quantifiable, however it should be considered for a working capital adjustment, as it is an obligation
from previous management and post-acquisition it may be treated as debt.
D.     Restricted cash: Depending on the full breakdown of restricted cash and its restrictions, some portions of restricted cash may be considered as part of working capital. This includes cash
that is restricted purely for operating activities, for example, in cash registers, which may be considered similar to a prepaid expense or deposit, which can be included in working capital.
E.     Key management salary: Current executives include Ellis Jacob (CEO), Gord Nelson (CFO), Dan McGrath (COO), Fabrizio Stanghieri (EVP & MD, Media), and Kevin Watts (EVP,
Exhibition & LBE). Total compensation for the FY23 period was $3.6 million, $1.5 million, $1.7 million, $719,000, and $696,000 for each executive respectively. Depending on any changes to
management’s go-forward compensation, an appropriate bonus and payroll accrual will need to be established. Refer to the appendix for an executive compensation analysis of comparable
companies.
