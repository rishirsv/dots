# QoE and earnings adjustments template

## Purpose
Reconcile reported earnings to a normalized, decision-useful measure (typically EBITDA), with defensible rationale.

## Minimum required elements

### 1) Bridge summary
Provide a reconciliation table:

Reported EBITDA  
+ / (–) Adjustments (by type)  
= Adjusted EBITDA

Include per adjustment:
- Description (short)
- Impact (numeric if known; otherwise “TBD”)
- Recurring vs non-recurring assessment
- Basis/source (what evidence supports it)

### 2) Adjustment rationale by type
Group adjustments into types so they are easy to review and defend. Example categories (tailor to the deal):
- Non-recurring items (one-time fees, settlements, restructuring)
- Run-rate normalization (known cost actions, hires, savings)
- Owner/related-party and discretionary items
- Accounting/classification items (reclasses impacting EBITDA definition)
- Other deal-specific items (clearly labeled)

### 3) Key sensitivities and open items
List what could materially move adjusted earnings:
- Data gaps
- Items under debate with management
- Timing/cut-off considerations

## Suggested exhibits
- Reported to adjusted bridge table (primary exhibit)
- Adjustment support schedule for the top 5 adjustments (appendix)

## Common pitfalls
- Adjustments without basis (creates credibility risk).
- Declaring items “non-recurring” without explaining why.
- Mixing EBITDA definitions across exhibits without noting it.
